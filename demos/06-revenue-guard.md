# Revenue Guard

Purpose: show Trasgo as a quote-governance layer for revenue operations and finance.

Command surface:

```bash
trasgo demo run revenue-guard
trasgo "run the revenue guard demo"
```

What it demonstrates:

- `ctx_context` confirmation: the quote state is measured in narrative tokens versus codec tokens.
- Compression proof: the guardrail decision survives a much smaller executable context.
- Functional gain: finance and deal desk collapse multiple review loops into one brief.
- Translating quote revisions into a compact §1 packet with discount, SLA, and cash-term deltas.
- Producing a guardrail decision before an unattractive deal shape reaches approval.
- Quantifying recovered gross profit and cash drag so finance and sales can negotiate from the same frame.

Why it matters:

- Operative value: standardizes quote triage for deal-desk teams.
- Economic value: protects margin, avoids unfunded service load, and reduces working-capital leakage.
